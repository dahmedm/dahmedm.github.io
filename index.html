About Me

I'm a PhD researcher in the AgRobotics Lab at Cornell University, working under Prof. Manoj Karkee. My research focuses on robotic perception and manipulation by integrating computer vision, 3D reconstruction, and machine learning for real-world task execution.

I am actively seeking internships and full-time opportunities in robotics.

Research Projects

Semantic-Enhanced 3D Reconstruction and Skeletonization of Orchard Trees

Dawood Ahmed, Martin Churuvija, Manoj Karkee
Developed a robotic perception pipeline integrating instance segmentation, multi-view point cloud fusion, and graph-based skeletonization for precise structural analysis of dormant trees. Semantically segmented RGB-D frames are fused via kinematics-guided ICP registration into a globally consistent, labeled 3D model. Semantic labels are preserved end-to-end throughout reconstruction and skeletonization, enabling class-conditioned geometric reasoning that extracts a smooth medial-axis graph with quantified morphological attributes including branch diameter and length.

Integrated Visual Servoing Framework for Robotic Pruning

Dawood Ahmed, Basit Muhammad Imran, Martin Churuvija, Manoj Karkee
Designed an integrated visual servoing framework that combines real-time perception with closed-loop control for precise robotic pruning in commercial orchards. The system integrates computer vision-based target identification with dynamic motion control, enabling accurate end-effector positioning for autonomous pruning operations in unstructured orchard environments.

Autonomous Bin Handling Robot for Orchard Logistics

Dawood Ahmed, Syed Usama Bin Sabir, Divyanth L.G, Priyanka Upadhyaya, Achyut Paudel, Robert Barragan, Apol Medrano, Osmar Alvarez, Bethany Navarro, Salvador Ayala, Dr. Safal Kshetri, Dr. Manoj Karkee
Led development of an autonomous mobile robot for orchard bin handling, featuring autonomous navigation in unstructured environments, vision-based bin alignment, and automated pick-and-place operations. The system addresses critical labor shortages in orchard logistics. Won Small Farm Tech Award ($5,000) in the Farm Robotics Challenge organized by UC Agriculture and Natural Resources.

Slicing-Aided Hyper Inference for Enhanced Fruit Bud Detection

Dawood Ahmed, Ranjan Sapkota, Martin Churuvija, Matthew Whiting, Manoj Karkee
Enhanced fruit bud detection during dormant season by implementing slicing-aided hyper inference techniques to improve detection of small objects in high-resolution orchard imagery. Achieved 80% precision and recall for bud detection, enabling accurate branch-level bud counting for early-season crop load estimation in commercial orchards.
Paper

Estimating Optimal Crop Load for Apple Orchards Using YOLOv8

Dawood Ahmed, Ranjan Sapkota, Martin Churuvija, Manoj Karkee
Developed a machine vision system using YOLOv8 to detect and count fruit buds on individual branches in commercial apple orchards. The system achieves high accuracy in complex orchard environments and enables branch-level crop load assessment, providing critical information for precision pruning decisions to optimize fruit quality and yield.
Paper

Semiautonomous Precision Pruning of Upright Fruiting Offshoot Orchard Systems

Alexander You, Nidhi Parayil, Josyula Gopala Krishna, Uddhav Bhattarai, Ranjan Sapkota, Dawood Ahmed, Matthew Whiting, Manoj Karkee, Cindy M. Grimm, Joseph R. Davidson
Research on a semiautonomous system for precision pruning of upright fruiting offshoot orchard systems. The system integrates various subsystems including vision-based perception for targeted pruning point determination, motion planning, and robotic manipulation to effectively prune modern planar orchard architectures.
